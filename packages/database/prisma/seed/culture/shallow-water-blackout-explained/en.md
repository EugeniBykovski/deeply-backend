# Shallow‑Water Blackout: What It Is

**TL;DR:** breath-hold blackout can happen **without panic**. The riskiest moment is often near the end of an attempt / during surfacing.

## The simple logic
- the “urge to breathe” is mainly driven by **CO₂**, not by critically low O₂;
- if CO₂ is suppressed (e.g., by hyperventilation), danger may not feel “loud.”

## Why hyperventilation is risky
Over-breathing lowers CO₂ → reduces urge to breathe → increases the chance of pushing too far.

## Core safety rules
- **Never train breath-hold in water alone.**
- Always use a **buddy** and an observation protocol.
- When in doubt, stop. “Just 10 more seconds” isn’t worth the risk.
