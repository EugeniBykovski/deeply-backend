# Frenzel: Equalization Basics

**TL;DR:** Frenzel equalization uses the tongue/throat as a “piston” rather than chest pressure. It’s more efficient and controllable at depth.

## Why Valsalva often limits depth
- relies on chest pressure,
- consumes air faster,
- becomes unreliable under strong compression.

## Frenzel logic
- move air higher; use tongue/soft palate control,
- less strain, more precision.

## Common mistakes
- neck/jaw tension,
- wrong tongue position,
- forcing pressure instead of a short, clean impulse.

## Gentle land practice
- short pressure “clicks” without strain,
- relaxed face awareness,
- nose‑pinch practice *without pain*.

## Deeply card idea
- Equalization = fine motor control: relaxation checklist
- One mistake → one fix
- Track quality (tension-free) rather than depth

**Note:** pain or congestion = stop and reassess.
