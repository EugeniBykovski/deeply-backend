# CO₂ & O₂ Tables: The Smart Way

**TL;DR:** tables train *comfort and control*, not “who can hold the longest.”

## What CO₂ tables train
- tolerance to rising CO₂ (urge to breathe),
- staying calm when it gets uncomfortable.

## What O₂ tables train
- operating at lower O₂ (carefully),
- relaxation efficiency and economy.

## Two safety rules
1) **Never do risky breath-hold experiments in water alone.**  
2) **No hyperventilation.** Over-breathing can suppress CO₂ signals and increase blackout risk.

## How to do it smart
- train dry (lying/sitting),
- rate attempts by *calm / control / comfort*,
- leave margin: consistency beats maxing out.

## Deeply format ideas
- Table timer with gentle cues (no aggressive goals)
- Post-session reflection: what did you feel? where did you tense?
- Streaks for consistency, not records

**Point:** tables are a self-regulation tool.
