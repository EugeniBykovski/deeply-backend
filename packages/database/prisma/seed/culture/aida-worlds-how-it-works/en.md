# AIDA Worlds: How Competitions Work

**TL;DR:** competitive freediving is rule‑driven and protocol‑heavy. “Freedom” is supported by a safety system.

## A typical competition day (high level)
- **Announcement:** athletes declare target depth/distance/time in advance.
- **Start window:** each athlete has a slot and preparation routine.
- **Dive + surface:** the critical moment is controlled surfacing.
- **Judging:** discipline rules + surface protocol evaluation.
- **Safety team:** people and gear ready to intervene instantly.

## Why this matters
- Competition teaches **planning** and prevents ego escalation.
- Performance is not bravery — it’s **repeatability under protocol**.

## Culture card ideas
- Announcements: why pre-declaring goals is powerful
- Surface protocol: what judges look for (and why)
- DNS/DQ: how rules keep the sport fair and safer

**Point:** rules aren’t limits — they’re the infrastructure of freedom.
