# Why Modern Sport Moved Away from No Limits

**TL;DR:** No Limits (sleds/lift systems) looks spectacular, but it’s heavily tech-dependent and far riskier — not a good foundation for a scalable sport.

## No Limits in one sentence
The athlete uses devices for descent/ascent. Depth increases, but the focus shifts from skill and physiology to engineering and risk management.

## Why it’s a poor competitive format
- **Hard to compare fairly:** too many technical variables.
- **Higher risk:** system failures, ascent speed, consciousness control.
- **Lower training clarity:** it’s not obvious what to “train” for sustainable progress.

## What modern freediving emphasizes instead
- **CWT/CNF/FIM and pool events:** more about technique, control, repeatability.
- **Safety systems:** standards, judging, protocols.

## Culture angles
- Sport vs spectacle: why rules “remove drama” to protect lives.
- Repeatability: what’s stronger — one headline dive or a season of consistent performances?

**Takeaway:** mature sports choose what can be done *safely, comparably, and regularly*.
